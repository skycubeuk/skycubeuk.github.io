Alkaptonuria (AKU) is a rare inborn error of metabolism caused by a deficiency of homogentisate 1,2-dioxygenase. This leads to the accumulation of homogentisic acid (HGA), which undergoes autoxidation to produce a dark, ochronotic pigment. This pigment deposits in connective tissues throughout the body, including cartilage, tendons, and heart valves, and is strongly associated with tissue degradation and joint damage.

While the macroscopic appearance of AKU pigmentation is striking, the precise chemical nature of the pigment species and the mechanism by which it causes tissue damage are not fully understood. By characterising *in vitro*-generated pigment using solid-state NMR spectroscopy and other analytical techniques, we aim to:

- Determine the molecular structure of the ochronotic pigment
- Understand the radical-based mechanism of collagen degradation
- Identify potential targets for clinical interventions to prevent or reverse pigmentation

This project is carried out in collaboration with Dr Brendan Norman at the Institute of Life Course and Medical Sciences, University of Liverpool.
