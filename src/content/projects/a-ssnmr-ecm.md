Solid-state NMR (ssNMR) spectroscopy is uniquely suited to investigating the extracellular matrix (ECM). Unlike solution NMR, ssNMR does not require the sample to be soluble — allowing us to study complex, heterogeneous biological materials in their native, hydrated state with minimal processing.

This project explores new applications of ssNMR methodology to ECM systems, including:

- Development of new pulse sequences optimised for ECM samples
- Application of dynamic nuclear polarization (DNP) enhancement to improve sensitivity on complex materials
- Characterisation of ECM components across tissues and disease states

The work underpins several of the lab's other research projects and develops the methodological toolkit available to the wider ECM research community.
