The extracellular matrix (ECM) undergoes significant changes with ageing and in cardiovascular disease. These changes — including increased collagen cross-linking, altered mineral composition, and post-translational modifications — contribute to tissue stiffening and reduced function.

By developing isotopic labelling protocols for mouse and mammalian cell culture systems, we can incorporate ^13^C and ^15^N labels into ECM proteins *in vivo* and *in vitro*. This enables solid-state NMR (ssNMR) spectroscopy to provide atomic-resolution structural information on the ECM in its functionally relevant hydrated state.

This long-standing collaboration with Prof Melinda Duer (University of Cambridge) has produced key insights into collagen post-translational modifications, the role of polyADP-ribose in biomineralisation, and the molecular basis of ECM changes in disease.
