*Schizosaccharomyces pombe* (fission yeast) is a well-established model organism in cell biology, particularly for studying the cell cycle and fungal biology. Its cell wall is a complex, highly organised structure composed primarily of β-glucans, α-glucans, and galactomannans.

By applying solid-state NMR (ssNMR) spectroscopy to intact cell wall preparations, we can quantify changes in cell wall composition and molecular organisation in response to specific genetic manipulations. This provides an atomic-level understanding of how genetic factors regulate cell wall architecture.

This project is carried out in collaboration with Prof Mohan Balasubramanian at the Centre for Mechanochemical Cell Biology, Warwick Medical School, and is funded by the Wellcome Trust.
