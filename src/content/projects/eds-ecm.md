Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (EDS) is a group of inherited connective tissue disorders caused by mutations in genes encoding extracellular matrix (ECM) proteins, primarily collagens and their modifying enzymes. Key symptoms include hypermobile joints, fragile or stretchy skin, and tissue fragility.

Using solid-state NMR (ssNMR) spectroscopy in combination with cell culture of human fibroblast cell lines from EDS patients, we can probe the molecular-level organisation of the ECM at atomic resolution. This approach allows us to identify structural defects that arise from the disease-causing mutations and understand how they translate to the macroscopic symptoms observed clinically.

This project is carried out in collaboration with Dr Darius Koester and Dr Sabeeha Malek at the Centre for Mechanochemical Cell Biology, Warwick Medical School.
